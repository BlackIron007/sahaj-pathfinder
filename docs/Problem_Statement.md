# Problem Statement: Sahaj PathFinder

**Tagline:** Every MSME Enters SBI Through a Different Door.

## Executive Summary

State Bank of India has invested significantly in digital MSME acquisition and servicing through platforms such as MSME Sahaj, SME Digital Loans, and YONO Business. Despite these massive infrastructure investments, acquiring MSMEs at scale remains a persistent challenge because businesses do not respond to a single, monolithic acquisition strategy.

The MSME market is heavily fragmented:

* Some MSMEs seek immediate working capital.
* Some are heavily influenced by Chartered Accountants and financial advisors.
* Some operate entirely within the gravity of large supply-chain ecosystems.
* Some prefer entirely digital, self-service onboarding.

Traditional acquisition systems focus exclusively on identifying *prospects*. The real challenge, however, is determining the most effective acquisition *path*. Sahaj PathFinder addresses this by discovering MSMEs through existing SBI ecosystems, understanding their likely motivations, identifying the most relevant SBI offering, and autonomously selecting the acquisition route mathematically most likely to convert them.

---

## The Problem: The "Who" vs. "How" Fallacy

Current acquisition processes operate with a fundamentally flawed, one-size-fits-all mindset. Banks are asking the wrong questions, leading to broken acquisition funnels.

| The Traditional Focus (The "Who") | The PathFinder Imperative (The "How") |
| --- | --- |
| Which MSMEs should we target? | Why would this specific MSME switch to SBI? |
| Which leads have the highest score? | Which acquisition route is most likely to succeed? |
| Which customers meet eligibility criteria? | Who influences their final banking decision? |

Because the current system ignores the "How," SBI experiences severe downstream friction:

* **Low conversion rates** on outbound marketing.
* **High customer acquisition costs (CAC)** due to manual effort.
* **Generic customer outreach** that gets ignored by business owners.
* **Missed ecosystem opportunities** hiding in plain sight.
* **Limited digital adoption** of flagship platforms like YONO Business.

---

## The Core Insight

**Every MSME enters SBI through a different door.** The fundamental challenge is not simply discovering more MSMEs. The challenge is dynamically determining the exact combination of variables required to pull them into the bank. To acquire successfully, the system must instantly calculate:

1. **Why** they would switch.
2. **What** SBI product is most relevant to their immediate pain point.
3. **Who** influences their financial decisions.
4. **Which** acquisition route has the lowest friction.
5. **When** the engagement should perfectly time their business cycle.

---

## Current Operational Challenges

The lack of intelligent routing manifests across three critical business pillars:

**1. Customer Acquisition**

* Severe lack of visibility into broader ecosystem relationships.
* Over-dependence on traditional, low-yield lead generation methods.
* Massive manual effort required for relationship managers to prioritize prospects.

**2. Digital Adoption**

* Low adoption rates of digital products despite their widespread availability.
* Generic, frustrating onboarding journeys that do not match the MSME's context.
* Zero personalization in the digital acquisition flow.

**3. Digital Engagement**

* Static, one-way acquisition campaigns.
* Poor contextual relevance to the MSME's daily operational realities.
* Weak engagement-to-conversion ratios.

---

## The Untapped Opportunity

SBI does not need to look outward to find its next 100,000 MSME customers; they are already interacting with the bank's infrastructure. SBI possesses access to incredibly valuable, yet siloed, ecosystem signals through:

* **MSME Sahaj** (Invoice data and counterparty ledgers)
* **SME Digital Loans**
* **YONO Business**
* **Transaction Networks** (UPI, NEFT, RTGS rails)
* **Existing Corporate Customers**
* **Supply Chain Relationships**

These ecosystems contain hidden acquisition opportunities that remain largely untapped. Instead of spending millions searching for new customers externally, SBI can intelligently identify and convert the opportunities already present within its own transactional gravity.

## Vision & Expected Impact

SBI should not treat every MSME as a cold lead; SBI must treat every MSME as a unique acquisition pathway. By discovering MSMEs, understanding their motivations, and selecting the highest-conversion route, Sahaj PathFinder delivers measurable impact across the board:

| Impact Area | Measurable Business Outcomes |
| --- | --- |
| **Customer Acquisition** | Discover previously unseen MSMEs, improve overall acquisition efficiency, and drastically reduce Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC). |
| **Digital Adoption** | Increase YONO Business daily active users, accelerate digital-first onboarding, and improve overall product utilization. |
| **Digital Engagement** | Deliver highly context-aware acquisition journeys, improve engagement quality, and secure higher conversion rates. |

## Conclusion

The future of MSME banking is not about finding more leads. The future is understanding which exact acquisition path is most likely to succeed for every individual business at exactly the right time. Sahaj PathFinder transforms SBI's strategy from volume-based lead generation into precision **Acquisition Intelligence**.
